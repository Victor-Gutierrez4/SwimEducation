Advanced Workouts

Challenging drills and training sets to increase speed, endurance, and stroke technique.

Take Your Training to the Next Level

Advanced swimmers need a balance of technique refinement, aerobic conditioning, and anaerobic power sets. The following drills are designed to push your limits and improve race readiness.

Advanced Drills

Swim multiple laps with reduced breathing intervals (e.g., breathe every 5, 7, or 9 strokes). Builds lung capacity and teaches calmness under stress.

Swim 200 meters, aiming to swim the second 100 faster than the first. Focuses on pacing and endurance while finishing strong.

Build sets like 100 IM, 200 IM, 300 IM, and back down. Focus on transitions, legal strokes, and consistent effort across all four strokes.

Train with Intensity

Advanced swimmers focus on speed, precision, and recovery. Every workout should challenge both your body and your mind to improve efficiency in the water.

Pro Tip: Use a waterproof pace clock or smart watch to track intervals and improve timing accuracy.
Warning: Hypoxic sets should only be done with supervision. Never push past your breath limit to the point of discomfort.
