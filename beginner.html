Beginner Workouts

Simple drills and techniques to help new swimmers build form and confidence in the water.

Why Start with the Basics?

Before jumping into full-length laps, mastering foundational techniques is essential. Beginner workouts focus on body position, breathing, and efficient movement to help you swim safely and confidently.

Beginner Drills

Practice your kick with a board. Focus on small, fast kicks from the hips—not the knees. Knees must be kept in a locked position and avoid bending them as you kick.

Start by placing the pull buoy snugly between your thighs to keep your legs afloat and minimize kicking. Focus on using your arms for propulsion, keeping a steady and smooth stroke as you swim. Maintain a streamlined body position to build upper body strength and improve your swimming technique.

Push off the wall in a tight streamline position. Both hands overhead and move in a dolphin motion from head to toe. Helps with balance and feel for the water.

Build Your Confidence

Learning to swim is not about speed at first—it's about staying calm, feeling balanced in the water, and being able to breathe smoothly while moving.

Tip: Always warm up before starting any swimming workout. A few stretches and a slow swim help avoid injury.
Safety Reminder: Never swim alone as a beginner. Always have supervision.
